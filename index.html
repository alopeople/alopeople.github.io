What is the likely impact?

One of the advantages of cloud service is cost saving. In a home enterprise, cloud service enables the company not to invest in similar physical resources. That is to say, the company does not need to spend money on its own server, and employees can use all the resources of the cloud to do more efficient work. The cloud features the ability to access another application over the network without actually downloading it, saving more disk space. On the other hand, when companies need more storage space, they only need to add a virtual server from the cloud, which is much easier than installing and configuring a server in real life. The downside of the cloud is the occasional loss of data. Of course, that's only in a few cases. Even so, most companies try to store key information themselves, rather than uploading it to the cloud.

Human-robot interaction

The state of the art in sensory intelligence for robots will have to progress through several orders of magnitude if we want the robots working in our homes to go beyond vacuum-cleaning the floors. If robots are to work effectively in homes and other non-industrial environments, the way they are instructed to perform their jobs, and especially how they will be told to stop will be of critical importance. The people who interact with them may have little or no training in robotics, and so any interface will need to be extremely intuitive. Science fiction authors also typically assume that robots will eventually be capable of communicating with humans through speech, gestures and facial expression, rather than an interface of command line. Although speech would be the most natural way for the human to communicate, it is unnatural for the robot.

What is the potential impact of this development?
1.) The Business Impact of Robotics on Our Lives

Robots have a remarkable impact upon business around our world. Human laborers are quickly finding themselves being replaced by robotic counterparts, who complete their jobs much more quickly and with near exact accuracy every time. This gives businesses a huge advantage in output, costs, and overall profitability. Robotic labour machines can produce vastly higher quantities than humans, creating higher supplies of finished goods. The robots present manufacturers with a savings in costs as well, paying much fewer wages to humans and paying slightly higher overhead costs for electricity and machine upkeep. With all these positives for robots in business, there are negatives as well. A common fear among many inventors and engineers is that, eventually, the human workforce will altogether be replaced by robotic machines and human labour will not be needed at all. These fears have been fueled by increasingly advanced robotic inventions in factories that have caused workers to be laid off, as well as commercial robotic products that complete simple labour tasks. Simple human labour tasks such as mowing grass and cleaning swimming pools have already been replaced by robots, and many more skilled labour positions, such as industrial parts manufacturers, have begun to switch to precision robot labour units.
